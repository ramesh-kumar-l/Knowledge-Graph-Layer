# 01 — Product Thesis

## The problem
Memory systems store facts but lose meaning. Memories accumulate as isolated records
with no shared identity, no relationships, and no way to answer *why* a fact is believed.
Retrieval becomes opaque: results appear without evidence, provenance, or confidence.

## The thesis
Knowledge only becomes useful when it is **connected and explainable**. A semantic
understanding layer — not just a graph store — is required to:
- Resolve memories into stable entities (people, projects, goals, concepts, …).
- Promote relationships to first-class, evidence-backed facts.
- Attach trust (confidence, evidence, provenance, verification, freshness) to every fact.
- Preserve history so knowledge can evolve without destroying the past.

## Why "understanding layer," not "graph DB"
A graph DB stores nodes and edges. This layer adds the semantics on top: identity
resolution, trust, provenance, versioning, and explainable retrieval. The graph is an
implementation detail; **trustworthy, auditable knowledge is the product.**

## Who it serves
Engineers, AI agents, enterprises, and future AI-native operating systems that need a
semantic memory they can reason over and *trust*.

## Success looks like
Any consumer can ask of any fact: *Why does this exist? What supports it? Where did it
come from? How trustworthy is it?* — and always get a grounded answer.
